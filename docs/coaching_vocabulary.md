# Coaching vocabulary

Short phrases we use a lot in training and games. Same words help players hear a cue quickly and know what to do.

---

## Phrases we use

| Phrase | What we mean |
|--------|----------------|
| **Soccer posture** | Athletic stance: knees soft, on the balls of the feet, ready to move. Often paired with scanning so the player is balanced and can go in any direction. |
| **Go back to go forward** | Sometimes you receive with your back to goal or under pressure; a safe touch backward or sideways (or a half-turn away from pressure) sets up the next forward pass or dribble. Do not force forward into traffic every time. |
| **Play the way you face** | If your hips and shoulders are already aimed one way, the simplest safe choice is often a pass or touch in that direction first; then re-shape if you need to switch direction. Reduces turnovers from trying to spin into pressure without a plan. |
| **Get connected** | Close enough to a teammate that you can pass on the ground with one or two touches, and angles make sense—not hiding in another zip code, not standing on top of each other. |
| **Be available** | Show for the ball: move into space, check toward or away, body open so the passer has a good option. If nobody is available, possession dies. |
| **Turn** | Receive across your body or with a touch that opens your hips so you can face forward and attack—or know before the turn that you will play forward or sideways right after. |
| **Man on** | A defender is closing; take a touch that protects the ball, play quicker, or lay it off—do not dwell. Often called by a teammate so the receiver hears it early. |
| **Cross** | Serve the ball from wide into the box or across dangerous areas for runners or the striker. |
| **Recycle** | Same idea as **cross** in our vocabulary: get the ball wide and serve it in, or re-use the wide channel to threaten again after a reset. |

## More common cues (add or edit as we standardize)

| Phrase | What we mean |
|--------|----------------|
| **Check to / check away** | Move toward the ball to receive, or peel off first to create space, then show. |
| **First touch** | Where the ball goes on contact—set up the next pass, turn, or shot; quality matters as much as receiving it. |
| **Scan / get your picture** | Look before the ball arrives so you know pressure, teammates, and space. |
| **Switch the play** | Move the ball from one side of the field to the other to stretch the defense and find space. |
| **Pressure** | Close down the player on the ball with controlled speed; force a mistake or backward pass. |
| **Cover** | Second defender: angle and distance behind the pressuring player so a line-splitting pass or dribble still gets challenged. |
| **Compact / squeeze** | Defenders slide as a group to shrink space in front of the ball. |
| **Track your runner** | When you mark someone or follow a run into the box on a set piece or open play, stay with them until the danger is over. |
| **Hold the line / step** | Back line moves together on offside timing or squeezes up when we want to compress space (context-specific). |
| **Clear it** | When there is no safe play, get the ball out of danger (wide, long, or high)—live to defend again. |
| **Reset** | Slow the tempo with a safe pass or dribble so the team can get shape back before going forward again. |
| **Support at an angle** | Offer a pass option diagonally in front, beside, or behind the ball so the passer has triangles, not straight lines only. |

## How we use this page

When we repeat the same phrases, players connect words to actions faster. If you introduce a new term in practice, add a one-line definition here so coaches and parents use the same language.
