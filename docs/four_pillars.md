# Four Pillars of Soccer
 
* mentality
* physical
* tactical
* technical  

## Mentality
* love for the game
* spirit of competition
* grit, determination, focus

Love for the game is ultimately the biggest determinant of long term success.  Players who love the game practice on the their own, come to practice ready to play, watch soccer on TV, pay attention the performance of the peers, and are always growing and learning.  If there is one thing we want to do is inspire love for the game.  

## Physical
* speed
* strength
* endurance
* balance

Physicality is the most closely related to natural ability, however, through practice, players will get faster and stronger.  Endurance is probably the most trainable of these aspects.  

## Tactical
* anticipation/ reading the game
* decision-making
* movement off the ball
* positioning, spacing, formations and roles
* communication and organization

Tactical ability and awareness must be experienced through well designed drills and small-sided games, learning the game through direct instruction, and by watching and studying the game on TV.  Tactical ability is about understanding how to create and deny opportunities. 

## Technical
* first touch and ball control
* passing accuracy and variety
* dribbling and close control
* shooting and finishing
* defensive technique

Technical skills are the tools a player uses to execute tactical plans.  These skills are built through deliberate, focused repetition and by practicing under game-like pressures.  Emphasize quality touches, gradual increase in speed, and transferring skills from drill to match situations.  Players can drastically improve their technical skills by practicing outside of formal practice.

## Well-Rounded Players
We see players with strengths and weaknesses across these dimensions of play.  Technical players with no grit, fierce competitors without tactics, fast players without ball skills.  By being aware of the strengths and weaknesses across these dimensions we can help to build well-rounded players.
