# Soccer Learning Matrix

This is a living map of skills: what we want every player to reach, what excellence looks like on top of that, and the common gaps that hold kids back. It ties to the [Four Pillars](./four_pillars.md) (mentality, physical, tactical, technical). The matrix will grow; we are starting with one technical thread that matters a lot at U12.

For each skill below, read across:

* **Team baseline** — the floor we coach toward so every kid can stay in the play.
* **Stretch / advanced** — extra layer for players who are ready; not required of everyone at once.
* **Gaps to watch** — habits we correct early so bad patterns do not stick.

---

## Technical

### Dribbling

**Team baseline.** Under pressure, a player can protect the ball, shift it to a safer foot or angle, dribble out of trouble, and **release a pass** to a teammate. The goal is not fancy moves; it is staying composed enough to keep possession and connect.

**Stretch / advanced.** Willing and able to **take on a defender** with change of pace and direction, sell a simple feint, and beat the defender cleanly when the moment is right—not every touch, but when the game rewards it.

**Gaps to watch.**

* Too predictable (same move, same side, same timing); defenders read it easily.
* Little or no dribble in traffic—only passes or only standing on the ball until it is stripped.
* Panic under pressure: head down, rushed touch, or **lashing the ball at a defender’s shins** instead of a composed dribble or pass.

Coaching note: build baseline first (close control, shielding, half-turn, pass after two or three touches). Add 1v1 beating defenders for players who have the baseline and good decision sense.
